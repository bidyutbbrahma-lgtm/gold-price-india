Gold Price FAQs

What is today’s gold price in India?

Today’s gold price in India is derived from international gold trading markets and converted into Indian Rupees based on prevailing currency exchange rates. This page displays updated gold prices per troy ounce, per gram, and per 10 grams to help users understand both global benchmarks and Indian retail pricing.

Why does the gold price change every day?

Gold prices change daily due to multiple global and domestic factors such as international demand and supply, movements in the US dollar, interest rate decisions by central banks, inflation expectations, and geopolitical events. Even small changes in global markets can influence daily gold prices in India.

Which gold price unit is commonly used in India?

In India, gold prices are traditionally quoted per 10 grams, especially for retail purchases and jewellery. However, international gold markets quote prices per troy ounce. This page shows both formats to provide complete transparency.

Is the gold price the same across all Indian cities?

Gold prices are broadly similar across India but may vary slightly between cities due to local taxes, transportation costs, jeweller margins, and state-level duties. The prices shown here represent benchmark market rates.

Is gold a good long-term investment in India?

Gold is widely regarded as a long-term store of value in India and is often used as a hedge against inflation, currency depreciation, and economic uncertainty. Many investors track daily gold prices before investing in physical gold, gold ETFs, or sovereign gold bonds.
